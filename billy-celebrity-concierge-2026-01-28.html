Where I Started

"I need a website"

I run a white-glove nightlife planning service in San Diego — birthdays, bachelor/ette parties, tourist nights. I leverage my promoter relationships to guarantee entry and handle all the logistics.

I came in thinking the next step was obvious: build a website, get more clients, grow the business.

Seemed straightforward.

2/5
What I Discovered

Two things changed everything

First: I am the product. My relationships, my judgment, my real-time problem-solving — that's what makes it work. And that doesn't scale.

Until I figure out what can be systematized vs. what stays founder-led, tech is just a distraction.

Second: My volume is way bigger than I thought.

The Real Opportunity

I'm not building a service business

I'm sitting on a distribution channel.

500+ people flowing through San Diego nightlife every month — and I control where they go.

The real play: own the flow, monetize both sides.

  • Venue partner subscriptions ($500-1,200/month)
  • Commissions on bookings (10%)
  • Client intake to own the data
  • My own monthly event ("The List") for recurring engagement
4/5
Where I'm At

Building the systems

Now focused on locking in the infrastructure that lets me scale without losing the trust and quality that made it work.

  • Venue partner subscription model (3-month contracts)
  • Client intake form to capture relationships
  • Core offer packages defined
  • Monthly hosted event in design
