The Founder's Week

December 11–19, 2025

// scroll to read

Starting Point

Coming off a good team meeting

We'd just announced the pivot to the team: LaunchMate isn't just about mentor-founder communication anymore. We're building a super-trusted personal agent for founders.

Team was on board. Everyone understood the shift. So heading into this week, we had alignment and momentum.

1/6

New Information

Then two things landed

First: We're only getting 4 users in the upcoming cohort. That's smaller than we'd hoped.

Second: There's an opportunity to open LaunchMate campus-wide—not just cohort founders, but anyone with a business idea.

So now we've got a decision to make.

2/6

The Trade-off

Two reasonable paths

Option A: Stay focused on the 4 cohort founders. Go deep. Test whether the "founder partner" hypothesis actually works.

Option B: Pivot to scale. Open it up campus-wide. Lean into what we know already works—the mentor value—and grow the user base.

Both are defensible. But they're very different bets.

3/6

Working Through It

The real question underneath

I talked it through with the business partner agent and the Friction Architect agent. And it came down to this:

The core thing we're trying to figure out is can founders actually feel known by an AI?

You can't answer that at scale. You answer it by going deep with a small number of people and seeing if the thing we're building actually lands.

4/6

Decision

Depth over breadth

We're staying focused on the 4.

Craig validated this in our mentor meeting. And then he added a new challenge: figure out how to productize growing an agent after LaunchMate jumpstarts it.

That's the next horizon. Once you've built trust with a founder, what's the path for that relationship to keep evolving?

5/6

Where We Are

End of week status

Team is working through Letta skills learning gaps. Still wrestling with an architecture question: agents, tools, memory blocks all add value—what's the right template for spinning up new agents?

But the big stuff is clear:

  • Experiment is set
  • Team is aligned
  • Question is sharper than it was Monday
