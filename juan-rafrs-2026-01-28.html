The Problem

When welding can't show up

In expeditionary environments — forward operating bases, ships at sea, remote locations — metal breaks and there's no way to fix it. No electricity, no machines, no consumables, no skilled welders.

I did 5+ customer discovery interviews with military folks. What I heard: ship welders often lack skills for certain repairs. Aluminum makes it worse — different machines, different skill sets. And the DMDV approval process is a major bottleneck.

The alternative to welding isn't better welding. It's nothing. Equipment stays broken.

2/4
The Insight

An 80-90% solution beats zero

RAFRS isn't competing with welding — it fills the gap where welding can't even show up.

Carbon-fiber cloth + adhesive composite system. Self-contained kit fits in an ammo can. ~15 min application, 8 hr cure, no specialized training required.

"An 80-90% solution is acceptable when the alternative is nothing."

Time is the critical constraint in field environments. Leadership trust and approval workflows matter as much as technical capability.

Unexpected signal: non-DoD interest emerging — people who can't afford welding or aren't near shops.

3/4
Current Status

Prototype complete, entering proof phase

Prototype v1 done: Demo kit in ammo can, tested on aluminum bike frame (T-shaped butt-welds). Now collecting strength and durability data.

Next: demos for welders to get expert feedback on use cases and limitations. Multiple interviewees already requested prototypes — real interest pending proof.

Team of 4 engineering students with military/ROTC backgrounds: Juan (lead), Melino Lopez, Lucy Contreras, Kevin Vu.

Ready to show rather than tell.
