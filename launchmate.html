LaunchMate Hypothesis Guide

What we think is true. How to test it. What signals to watch for.

The Fog You're In

You've built an AI agent for founders. Some people seem interested. But you don't know:

  • Why aren't more people engaging?
  • What's actually blocking adoption?
  • Is this a product problem or a positioning problem?
  • What should you fix first?

This page is your flashlight. It won't give you answers — it will tell you exactly what questions to ask and what signals to look for.

The Core Hypothesis

Primary hypothesis
Founders don't believe AI can truly know them — and even when it helps, they can't see the value.

This is a trust + meaning problem, not a feature problem.

If this is true, adding more features won't help. You need to prove you know them instantly, then show value over time.

How to test this

Talk to 5 founders who tried LaunchMate but didn't stick.

Ask: "What made you stop using it?" Listen for trust/meaning language vs. feature requests.

Strong signal it's true
  • "It felt generic, like talking to ChatGPT"
  • "I wasn't sure what it was for"
  • "I didn't know if it was actually helping"
  • "It didn't really know my situation"
Signal it's wrong
  • "It was missing [specific feature]"
  • "The UI was confusing"
  • "I didn't have time" (probe deeper — time is usually a proxy)
"If 3 out of 5 churned users mention trust or meaning, you've validated the hypothesis. Now you know what to fix."

Runner-Up Hypotheses

If the primary hypothesis is wrong, test these:

2

Identity Friction

"I'm the visionary — I don't need AI telling me what to do."

3

Status Friction

"Using AI for help feels like weakness vs. founders who figure it out."

4

Timing/Trigger Friction

"I don't know when to use it — it's not part of my flow."

5

Context Gap

"It doesn't know what my mentor said, my demo day timeline, my real blockers."

Note: These might all be true. But you need ONE to focus on first. The primary hypothesis is the starting point.

Interview Script

When you talk to founders, use these questions:

Opening: "You tried LaunchMate for a bit. Can you walk me through what happened?"
Probe trust: "Did it feel like the agent actually understood your situation? What was missing?"
Probe meaning: "Was it clear what you were supposed to use it for? What did you expect vs. what you got?"
Probe identity: "How did it feel to ask an AI for help with your startup?"
Probe triggers: "When would you have wanted to use it? What would have reminded you?"
Magic wand: "If it could do one thing perfectly, what would make you use it every day?"
Close: "What would it take for you to try it again?"

Validation Checklist

Before you build anything new, complete this:

  • Talk to 5 founders who churned or went inactive
  • Categorize each response: Trust / Meaning / Identity / Timing / Features / Other
  • Look for patterns: Do 3+ mention the same friction?
  • Write down exact quotes (not your interpretation)
  • Identify the ONE friction to solve first
  • Brainstorm 3 lightweight experiments to address it
  • Run smallest possible test before building

If Trust + Meaning Is Confirmed

Here's the unlock we've hypothesized:

1

Prove you know them instantly

First interaction should reflect something specific about THEM — not generic advice.

2

Show value over time

Track decisions, surface patterns, remind them of their own progress. "You mentioned X three weeks ago — did it work?"

3

Make the mirror visible

"Your agent sees patterns you're too close to notice." The insight IS the product.

Test before building: Can you manually deliver this experience to 3 founders and see if it changes their engagement?

What "Validated" Looks Like

You've validated the hypothesis when:
  • 3+ of 5 interviews point to the same friction
  • You can predict what a new churned user will say
  • A small intervention addressing that friction changes behavior
  • Founders describe the problem back to you in their own words
You've invalidated it when:
  • Responses are scattered across many different issues
  • Fixing the hypothesized friction doesn't change engagement
  • Founders say "that's not really it" when you reflect the hypothesis back

Remember

This friction map is a hypothesis generator, not a prediction engine.

  • It tells you what might be true

  • It does NOT tell you what is true

The map gets you out of the fog. The interviews get you to truth.
