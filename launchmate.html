The Fog

You've built something. But you don't know why it's not working.

People try it. Some seem interested. But they don't stick.

Is it the product? The positioning? Something you can't see?

This guide won't give you answers. It will tell you exactly what to ask.

1/10

The Hypothesis

We think this is the core blocker

Primary hypothesis
Founders don't believe AI can truly know them — and even when it helps, they can't see the value.

This is trust + meaning, not features.

If true, adding more features won't help. You need to prove you know them instantly, then show value over time.

2/10

The Test

Talk to 5 founders who stopped using it

5
churned founders to interview

Not active users. Not fans. The ones who left.

Ask them one thing: "What made you stop?"

Listen for trust/meaning language vs. feature requests.

3/10

What to Listen For

Signals the hypothesis is right

You'll hear things like
  • "It felt generic, like talking to ChatGPT"
  • "I wasn't sure what it was for"
  • "I didn't know if it was actually helping"
  • "It didn't really know my situation"

If 3 of 5 say something like this, the hypothesis is validated.

4/10

Or Maybe Not

Signals the hypothesis is wrong

If you hear this instead
  • "It was missing [specific feature]"
  • "The UI was confusing"
  • "I just didn't have time"

If responses are scattered, or focused on features, the hypothesis is wrong.

That's fine. Now you test the next one.

5/10

Backup Hypotheses

If #1 is wrong, test these

  • Identity: "I don't need AI telling me what to do"
  • Status: "Using AI feels like weakness"
  • Timing: "I don't know when to use it"
  • Context: "It doesn't know my real situation"

These might all be true. But you need ONE to focus on first.

6/10

Interview Script

What to ask them

  • "Walk me through what happened when you tried it"
  • "Did it feel like it understood your situation?"
  • "Was it clear what you were supposed to use it for?"
  • "How did it feel to ask AI for help?"
  • "If it could do one thing perfectly, what would make you use it daily?"
7/10

If Validated

Here's the unlock

1. Prove you know them instantly.
First interaction reflects something specific about THEM — not generic advice.

2. Show value over time.
Track decisions, surface patterns. "You mentioned X three weeks ago — did it work?"

3. Make the mirror visible.
"Your agent sees patterns you're too close to notice."

8/10

Important

This is a hypothesis, not a prediction

The friction map tells you what might be true.

It tells you what questions to ask.

It does NOT tell you what IS true.

The map gets you out of the fog. The interviews get you to truth.

9/10

The One Thing

Talk to 5 churned founders this week

  • Find 5 who tried and stopped
  • Ask what made them leave
  • Listen for trust/meaning vs. features
  • Count: do 3+ say the same thing?
  • Write down their exact words

Everything else is guessing.
