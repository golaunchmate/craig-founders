Breaking the Chicken-and-Egg

December 2025 // scroll to read

Starting Point

Running a dual-track test

Came into the week running a dual-track validation approach—QR code tests alongside Instagram content.

Trying to see which channel could actually move students toward restaurant deals.

Pretty quickly realized the QR approach was too heavy. Not enough signal for the effort.

So I killed it.

1/5

Early Win

All-in on Instagram

Freed up to go all-in on Instagram. The results were encouraging:

300+
Views on content

Reach is there. Students are seeing the content.

But then I hit a wall...

2/5

The Wall

Views don't mean anything

Views don't mean anything if there's no action to take.

I caught myself being "passive" because Foodie doesn't have a restaurant deal to actually offer yet.

Classic chicken-and-egg problem: need students to attract restaurants, need restaurants to attract students.

3/5

The Pivot

Flipped the script

Instead of waiting around for a partner to magically appear, I flipped the script.

Now testing a "fake door"—posting content that asks students to DM for early access to deals that don't exist yet.

If people actually DM, that's real demand signal I can take directly to restaurants and say "look, I have X students ready to go."

Smart lean move that breaks the chicken-and-egg.

4/5

Where I Am Now

Watching for signals

  • Got reach: 300+ views ✓
  • Testing demand: fake door DMs live
  • Working network: 3 businesses contacted

Winter break is my window to lock down that first restaurant partner.

Signals I'm watching:

DM count from the fake door test + responses from the 3 businesses.

5/5
