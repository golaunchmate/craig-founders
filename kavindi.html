Breaking the Chicken-and-Egg

December 2025 // scroll to read

Starting Point

Running a dual-track test

Came into the week running a dual-track validation approach—QR code tests alongside Instagram content—trying to see which channel could actually move students toward restaurant deals.

But pretty quickly I realized the QR approach was too heavy. Not enough signal for the effort.

So I killed it.

1/5

Early Win

Going all-in on Instagram

That freed me up to focus entirely on Instagram, and the results were encouraging:

300+
views on content

Proof that students are paying attention. The reach is there.

2/5

The Problem

Views don't mean anything yet

But then I hit a wall: views don't mean anything if there's no action to take.

I caught myself being "passive" because Foodie doesn't have a restaurant deal to actually offer yet.

Classic chicken-and-egg problem: need deals to attract students, need students to attract restaurants.

3/5

The Pivot

Flipped the script with a fake door

Instead of waiting around for a partner to magically appear, I flipped the script.

Now testing a "fake door"—posting content that asks students to DM for early access to deals that don't exist yet.

If people actually DM, that's real demand signal I can take directly to restaurants: "Look, I have X students ready to go."

Smart lean move that breaks the chicken-and-egg.

4/5

Where I Am Now

Winter break = first partner

  • Reach: 300+ views ✓
  • Demand test: fake door DMs running
  • Pipeline: 3 businesses contacted (waiting on responses)

Winter break is my window to lock down that first restaurant partner.

Watching: DM count from fake door test + responses from the 3 businesses.

5/5
