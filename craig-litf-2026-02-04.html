The Starting Point

Time Travelers Who Weren't Using Their Superpower

Technical founders with early AI access are experiencing problems 12-36 months before mainstream users.

They're living in the future. But most weren't capitalizing on it.

So we built Live in the Future: a 10-week curriculum to systematize frontier observation.

  • Immerse → Observe → Extrapolate → Build → Stress Test
  • Friction Logs to capture real pain
  • Inevitability Arguments to separate signal from noise
2/5

The Problem

Founders Kept Drifting Incremental

Even at the frontier, they kept building:

"AI-powered SaaS"
"Like [existing product] but with AI"

They were building on layers that AI is actively commoditizing.

Being at the frontier wasn't the problem. They were on the wrong layer of it.

3/5

The Framework

Inversion: "If AI makes X trivial, what becomes valuable?"

Commoditization

What was hard is now easy (writing software, building SaaS)

Emergence

What was low-value is now critical (agent training, AI evaluation)

Bottleneck

AI solves X, new problem Y emerges (can write code → can't trust code)

4/5

The Four Questions

Our Standard Pushback

  • What layer are you on? Will it exist in 2 years?
  • What's your inversion bet?
  • What's the new bottleneck?
  • Who still has a job?

4 teams, 10 founders now running through this. The Inversion Framework got its own specialist agent to stress-test them.

"I thought the challenge was getting founders to the frontier.
I realized the real challenge is getting them to the right layer—the one that will still exist after AI commoditizes everything around it."
