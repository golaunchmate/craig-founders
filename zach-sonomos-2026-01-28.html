1/5
The Problem

Shadow AI is uninsurable

I'm a J.D./M.B.A. candidate, so I see AI privacy from the legal side. Professionals are pasting sensitive client data into ChatGPT every day — PII, financials, privileged communications.

Here's the kicker: your malpractice insurance won't cover AI breaches. I talked to cyber liability carriers and they confirmed it — shadow AI is a major uninsurable risk.

That's the pain point. Not abstract "privacy concerns" — concrete "you're exposed and no one will cover you."

2/5
The Pivot

Finding the real buyer

I started pitching startups. They didn't care — move fast, break things, privacy is someone else's problem.

Then I tried investors. Lukewarm.

The breakthrough: regulated professionals in mid-size firms. Especially law firms. They have real liability, real compliance requirements, and real fear.

I pitched at an AI governance panel at my law school — 15-20 people came up after asking "is it ready?" and handed me their LinkedIn. The dean wants to feature me in the alumni newsletter.

3/5
The Insight

Insurance carriers = distribution

Here's what I didn't expect: the insurance carriers who told me shadow AI is uninsurable? They expressed "a ton of interest" in Sonomos.

They want firms to mitigate AI risk. If my tool reduces their exposure, they might actually push it to their clients.

"Insurance carriers are both validation source AND potential distribution channel."

That's the play I'm building toward — but first, I need to ship and prove it works.

4/5
