The Realization

They Want to Build the Obvious Thing

"I thought the hard part would be getting founders to the frontier. I realized the hard part is keeping them there when they naturally want to build the obvious thing."

Every team came in wanting to build something. My job is to make sure they're building on a layer that will still exist in two years.

The Inversion Framework forces that question before they commit.

2/5

What Surprised Me

Four Lessons from Week 1

  • Inversion worked immediately — Jai & Satith saw their 3D direction was commoditizing. Frustration = founders thinking harder.
  • Not all teams need "pure LITF" — Think Tank's sports domain is fine; the capability exploration is what matters.
  • Technical level determines the experiment — Less technical? "Feed clips to GPT-4V and document where it breaks." Same frontier, different entry.
  • One team landed naturally — Samuel came in curious about autonomous agents. No pushing needed.
