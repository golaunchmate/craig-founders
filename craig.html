Craig's Week

From Interview Questions to "Make Me Feel Known"

December 2025 // scroll to read

Starting Point

Standard onboarding approach

Started testing my own founder agent with a typical onboarding flow. The agent had processed my documents, identified gaps, and was ready to walk through structured questions.

Confirmed the basics, then stopped it cold:

"Before we go any further, it's important that you make me feel like you know me. How would you do that?"
1/6

The Pivot

Synthesizing, not collecting

I wasn't interested in answering interview questions. I wanted proof that the agent was synthesizing, not just collecting.

The agent took a risk—reflected back what it noticed without me saying it: that I'm testing whether the Launchmate promise (AI that truly knows you) actually works, by being the user.

It nailed it. But I pushed further:

"Instead of interview questions, we need a tick-tock. You share something about me, that causes you to ask more."
2/6

What Emerged

30 years of communication tech

The tick-tock dialogue unlocked my whole story:

Eudora → early internet email
Qualcomm → wireless messaging
Launchmate → AI as communication infrastructure

The through-line: every time a new technology layer emerges, I ask "what do humans need to say to each other through this thing?"

3/6

The Puzzle

Asymmetric communication

The current puzzle: asymmetric communication in mentor/founder relationships.

Mentors want to stay in the loop. But founders experience updating them as work.

We landed on a framework of three relationship types:

  • NEAR — frequent enough to stay synced naturally
  • FAR — infrequent enough to start fresh each time
  • MIDDLE — the underserved zone

Mentor/founder sits in that middle zone.

4/6

The Breakthrough

Continuity for relationships that don't have natural continuity

That's the insight. That's what Launchmate—and specifically the Transcript Agent architecture—is solving for.

Relationships that meet too infrequently to stay synced, but too frequently to start from scratch.

The agent provides the continuity that the relationship cadence can't.

5/6

Where I Am Now

Onboarding approach validated

  • Tick-tock dialogue > linear questions
  • Core product insight articulated
  • Founder profile now rich with context
  • Trust established with my own agent

Next: deeper work on Transcript Agent design—what each side (founder/mentor) actually needs to receive.
