Craig's Week

From Interview Questions to Tick-Tock

January 2026 // scroll to read

Starting Point

Testing my own Founder Agent

Started the week by doing what we ask founders to do: use my own agent.

First attempt was interview-style questions. The agent asking me things, me answering.

It felt wrong. Too formal. Too much like filling out a form. Not how I actually think.

1/5

The Pivot

Tick-tock dialogue

Discovered a better pattern: tick-tock.

Agent reflects something back. Asks what it opens up. I riff on it. Agent reflects again.

Back and forth. Like jazz. The agent isn't interrogating me—it's thinking alongside me.

That's the interaction model that works.

2/5

What Surfaced

30-year through-line

The tick-tock process surfaced something I hadn't articulated:

Eudora Email archaeology
Qualcomm Communication infrastructure
LaunchMate Relationship continuity

I'm a communication archaeologist. That's been the thread all along.

3/5

The Insight

The middle zone problem

Identified the core problem LaunchMate solves:

Some relationships are too infrequent to stay synced but too frequent to start from scratch each time.

That awkward middle zone. You know each other, but you've lost the thread.

"Continuity for relationships that don't have natural continuity."

That's it. That's what we're building.

4/5

Where I Am Now

Eating our own cooking

  • Interview questions → tick-tock dialogue
  • Surfaced 30-year through-line I hadn't articulated
  • Clarified core value prop: continuity for the middle zone
  • The agent process itself proved the concept

Used our own tool. Found something real. The loop maintains itself.

5/5
