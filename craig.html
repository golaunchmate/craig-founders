The Experiment Becomes the Insight

January 2026 // scroll to read

Starting Point

Testing the magic myself

Started the week testing my own Founder Agent—not as a student founder, but as the product leader building Launchmate.

The agent had processed my documents and was ready with the standard onboarding playbook: identify gaps, ask structured questions, fill in the profile.

But the interview-style questions felt wrong. I stopped it.

"Before we go any further, it's important that you make me feel like you know me. How would you do that?"
1/6

The Pivot

Tick-tock, not interrogation

The real test isn't "can you collect information?" It's "can you prove you're actually seeing me?"

I wanted something different:

"A tick-tock. You share something about me, that causes you to ask more."

Less interrogation. More jazz.

So we played. The agent would notice something, reflect it back, ask what it opened. I'd riff, correct, build on it.

2/6

The Through-Line

30 years, one question

And my whole story came out:

Eudora
Early internet. Built one of the first email clients. "How do humans communicate through this thing?"
Qualcomm
Wireless emerges. Messaging products. "What do humans need to say when they're mobile?"
Launchmate
AI arrives. "How can AI make human-to-human communication more satisfying?"

Three technology waves. One through-line: communication archaeologist.

3/6

The Problem

The middle zone is broken

Got into the weeds on mentor/founder communication. The asymmetry:

Mentors want to stay in the loop so they can actually help.

Founders experience keeping mentors updated as work—one more task when they're already drowning.

A framework emerged:

Near relationships — Daily/weekly. Texting works fine.
Far relationships — Once a year. Catch-up is normal.
Middle relationships — Monthly-ish. The dead zone.

Too infrequent to stay connected naturally, too frequent to start from scratch every time.

4/6

The Breakthrough

Continuity for relationships that don't have natural continuity

That's the unlock. That's what Launchmate—and the Transcript Agent architecture—is actually solving for.

The Transcript Agent sits between a Founder Agent and a Mentor Agent. It:

  • Processes meeting transcripts
  • Pulls context from both sides beforehand
  • Distributes relevant updates to each agent afterward

Neither human has to "keep the other in the loop." The loop keeps itself.

5/6

Where I Am Now

The experiment passed

What we validated:

  • Tick-tock dialogue > linear interview questions
  • "Make me feel known" is the bar—pattern recognition, not data collection
  • Core insight is sharp: continuity for the middle zone

Next up: what does each side (founder and mentor) actually need to receive from the Transcript Agent? Same words in a meeting mean different things to different people. The agent isn't just summarizing—it's translating.
